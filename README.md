# Airbnb Market Analysis Dashboard — Power BI

An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing **2,79,712 Airbnb listings** across **10 global cities** 
from **2008 to 2020**, built to uncover pricing patterns, host performance, and guest satisfaction trends.

**Dataset:** 1,82,024 Hosts | 5,373K Reviews | 144 Property Types

---

## 📊 Dashboard Pages

### 1. Overview
- New listing trends from 2008–2020 across 6 phases: Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline → Reinvention → COVID-19
- Room type breakdown: Entire place, Hotel room, Private room, Shared room
- Airbnb reached peak listings in **2015**, became profitable in second half of **2016**
- Growth halted in **2019** due to COVID-19

### 2. Ratings
- Paris, NYC, and Sydney account for nearly **half of all listings and 48% of total reviews**
- Hotel rooms are the most expensive at **$800 avg/night**; Entire place at **$673**, Shared room **$580**, Private room **$462**
- **Mexico City and Rio de Janeiro** are the overall best-rated cities
- **Hong Kong and Istanbul** score the lowest overall
- Cleanliness and Value for Money are the two metrics scoring lowest across cities

### 3. Reviews & Trust
- **96.4%** of customers wrote a review only once; **98.3%** wrote 3 times or less
- One customer wrote **283 reviews** (highest recorded)
- Paris and Rome dominate review shares from **April to August**, reflecting peak European summer travel
- New York sees review spikes in **November and December** during the holiday season
- Over **two-thirds** of Airbnb hosts are fully verified; nearly all provide at least one trust signal

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Airbnb peaked at **~40K new listings in 2015**, followed by regulatory tightening in 2016–2017
- **Hotel rooms cost nearly twice** the price of private rooms ($800 vs $462 avg/night)
- **Paris is the city with the most listings and reviews** across all 10 markets
- The vast majority of guests (**96.4%**) are one-time reviewers, suggesting low repeat booking rates per listing
